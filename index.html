LiveTrace

Draw any picture in a combined virtual and augmented reality environment!

New
Although it IS fun to roam around a virtual world on the Oculus Rift, we believe that virtual reality is at it's coolest when you can interact with real-life objects as well. LiveTrace AVR is an example of that vision and is meant to bring out the artist in you. We use the Oculus Rift to transfer you to a beautiful virtual environment that includes a sketchpad on which you can express your art. The cool thing is, the sketchpad also exists in real life - this means while you are having fun drawing in virtual reality, you are actually creating a real drawing too.

Here's how it works:
Take a picture of something inspiring that you want to draw and upload it using the LiveTrace AVR iOS app. The picture is automatically placed into your computer where you need to drag and drop the picture into the LiveTrace Mathematica application for image processing. Then, pick a virtual environment to teleport to, grab a sketchpad, set up the webcam, and put the Oculus Rift on. The picture you took and processed appears as an overlay on your virtual sketchpad, which you can trace using a real marker on your real sketchpad, all while enjoying the view of a beautiful virtual environment.

This project won the Wolfram Sponsor Prize at Hack the Anvil 2015.

CodeView

This hack allows people to practice their technical interviewing skills on a synchronized collaborative coding platform that allows people to test code in their browser as well as communicate using VoIP. The service is completely gamified and everyone is ranked on a leaderboard that is based on a system similar to ELO.

This project won the Michigan ITS Prize for Most Innovative Hack for Teaching or Learning as well as the Microsoft Sponsor Prize at MHacks 5.

EPICS ISBVI Research

I created the website for the EPICS ISBVI research group at Purdue University of which I am a part of. We are trying to create many technologies to modernize the world of the blind and visually impaired, including connecting blind students' typewriters to the internet and creating a magnifying system to magnify textbooks and other objects to help those that are visually impaired.

This project won the William P. Corocan Award for Excellence in Human-Centered Design in May 2015.

ShakeToShare

ShakeToShare is a way to send contact information by shaking hands (using Pebble smartwatches and Android) and was created at Wildhacks 2014. The hack can also be modified to send payment information by venmo or other technologies by using different gestures (this wasn't implemented at the hackathon due to the time constraint).
